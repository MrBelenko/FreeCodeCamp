Here's a time line of Oliver Napoleon Hill life:

  • 1883 26 October - Hill accepted a job working for the lawyer Rufus A. Ayers, a coal magnate and former Virginia attorney general.
  • 1901 - Born in Pound, Virginia.
  • 1907 - Hill relocated to Mobile, Alabama and co-founded the Acree-Hill Lumber Company.
  • 1909 - Hill relocated to Washington D.C. and initiated the "Automobile College of Washington," where he instructed students to build, chauffeur and sell motor cars.
  • June 1910 - While managing his automobile college, Hill married his first wife, Florence Elizabeth Horner.
  • 1928 - Hill relocated to Philadelphia and convinced a Connecticut-based publisher to publish his eight-volume work The Law of Success. The book was Hill's first major success, allowing Hill to adopt an opulent lifestyle.
  • 1935 - Hill's wife Florence filed for a divorce in Florida.
  • 1937 - Hill published the best-selling book Think and Grow Rich, which became Hill's best-known work.
